Dip trigger iHow far below its trailing average the NAV must fall before Dynamic buys more. At −10%: if the fund is 10% below its 6-month average, Dynamic increases its investment. Lower = more sensitive; higher = only reacts to real crashes. Example: avg ₹100, NAV ₹89 → −11% → trigger fires. −10%
Rise trigger iHow far above the trailing average the NAV must rise before Dynamic reduces its fund investment and parks the excess. Same logic as dip trigger but in reverse. Example: avg ₹100, NAV ₹111 → +11% → trigger fires, Dynamic buys less. +10%
Avg window iHow many past months of NAV are averaged to form the trigger baseline. 6 months = recent momentum reference. 12 months = slower baseline that ignores short-term swings. Uses only historical prices — no look-ahead. Lower = more signals and more switching. 6 months
Step per trigger iEach additional full multiple of the trigger threshold adds this step to the SIP multiplier. At 10% step: one threshold crossed = ×1.10, two = ×1.20, etc. Higher step = more aggressive sizing on big moves. Most users don't need to change this. 10%

How It Works
Equal capital both strategies — max(dynAmt, baseSIP) each month. The strategy investing less parks the gap in liquid at your rate.

▼ Dip: NAV below trailing avg → Dynamic buys more.
▲ Rise: NAV above trailing avg → Dynamic buys less, parks rest.

Avg window uses past months only (no look-ahead). XIRR computed via Newton-Raphson on time-dated cashflows.
